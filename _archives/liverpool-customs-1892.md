This customs record from the Port of Liverpool documents the import of 847 elephant tusks from East Africa, providing valuable data about the scale and nature of the late Victorian ivory trade.

## Document Description

The manifest details the cargo of the steamship *Doreen Castle*, arriving from Zanzibar via Aden. The ivory shipment is listed among other colonial imports including cloves, coconut oil, and coffee.

## Key Information

- **Total Weight:** 12,340 pounds of ivory
- **Number of Tusks:** 847 pieces
- **Consignee:** Harrison & Sons, Liverpool

## Historical Significance

Liverpool was the primary port of entry for ivory into Britain during the 19th century. Records like this one help us understand:

- The volume of ivory flowing into European markets
- The merchants and trading companies involved
- The economic value of the ivory trade
- Connection to the broader network of colonial trade routes

## Research Value

Combined with other sources, customs records allow researchers to estimate total ivory imports over time and track changes in the trade. This particular manifest coincides with the period of most intensive ivory extraction from East Africa.
