Our scientific testing program employs state-of-the-art analytical methods to determine the species, age, and geographic origin of ivory materials. These techniques are crucial for conservation efforts, legal enforcement, and historical research.

Isotopic Analysis

Stable isotope ratios (carbon, nitrogen, oxygen, strontium) provide information about geographic origin and diet, helping to identify the region where an elephant lived.

DNA Analysis

Genetic testing can identify species (African vs. Asian elephant, mammoth), population, and even individual elephants through reference databases.

Spectroscopy

Raman and FTIR spectroscopy help distinguish ivory from substitute materials and can provide information about age and treatment history.

Radiocarbon Dating

Carbon-14 dating determines when an elephant died, distinguishing between antique ivory and recently poached material.
